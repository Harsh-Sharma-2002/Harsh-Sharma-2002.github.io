About

I am a machine learning and systems-oriented engineer driven by one core idea: intelligent systems should be architected, not improvised. My work focuses on building structured, controllable, and reusable AI pipelines that go beyond experimentation and into real-world applicability.

I am particularly interested in retrieval systems, agent architectures, and the integration of large language models into developer tooling. Projects like RepoScope and Evi-Agent reflect my interest in explicit control flow, hybrid memory systems, and policy-driven decision logic — treating AI systems as engineered software, not black-box demos.

Alongside systems work, I explore applied machine learning in healthcare and vision. From RoBERTa-based medical transcription classification to self-supervised Masked Autoencoders for dental radiographs, I enjoy working on problems where data constraints and model design intersect.

My long-term goal is to build robust AI infrastructure — systems that combine machine learning, backend engineering, and architecture-first thinking. I want to work on problems where intelligence must be reliable, interpretable, and scalable.
